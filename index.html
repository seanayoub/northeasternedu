Raising a Reader

Looking ahead from a year in review.

Where we've been, where we're going.

At Raising a Reader, our mission is to close the literacy opportunity gap among children ages birth to six in under-resourced communities by helping families develop and practice shared home reading habits.

More than thirty-five years of research shows that being regularly read to by a caregiver before Kindergarten is a significant factor impacting a child’s academic success. In Massachusetts, a child’s reading comprehension is tested for the first time in third grade, and the results are widely accepted by researchers as a predictor of both high school successes and college enrollment.

Results from the 2023 Massachusetts Comprehensive Assessment System (MCAS) prove that third graders are not reading proficiently, deeply impacting those in under-resourced communities.

In point, this visualization will illustrate the difference between the ratio of books to children in middle income versus low income communities when toggled. In middle income communities, the ratio is 13 books to 1 child - in low income communities, it's 1 book to 300 children.

The most significant factor influencing a child’s early educational success is an introduction to books and being read to at home prior to beginning school. This is where we can help- by funding Raising a Reader's programs, children will receive the hands on support they need to read, learn, and thrive.

Children learn best when they are actively involved in the process. Join us in our mission to bring the gift of literacy to even more children and their families this year.
